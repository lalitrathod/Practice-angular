Q: What is the difference between constructor and ngOnInit?

A: Constructor is called when the class is instantiated (before Angular processes the component). ngOnInit is called after Angular has initialized the component's data-bound properties. Use ngOnInit for initialization logic that depends on Angular's initialization.

Q: What are Signals in Angular?

A: Signals (introduced in Angular 16) are a reactive primitive that can hold a value and notify consumers when that value changes. They provide fine-grained reactivity and better performance.
